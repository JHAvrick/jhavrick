In any case, if you'd like to summon *me*, you can try the mirror thing or you could just use this blog's [contact form](/contact), email me at josh@avrick.dev (preferred), or send me a message on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joshavrick/).

In the meantime, let me tell you about some of the work I've been doing. You see, when I'm not memorizing passages from my copy of ["The Mythical Man Month"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Mythical_Man-Month), I am working full-time (remotely) at a company called [Alertus](https://www.alertus.com/), based in Baltimore, where we build emergency alerting software, hardware, you name it, *the works*. Here is an incomplete list of some of the projects for which I have had ownership:

 - <strong>Mass Notification Recipient, a Chrome Extension</strong> - This early project involved the design and development of a new product from scratch, a Chrome extension, which could be deployed by enterprise customers via GSuite (now Google Workspace) or GPO, usually to a fleet of Chromebooks. <!-- You'll find some [articles](/articles) on this blog about this. -->

 - <strong>IPAWS Activation and Reporting, an Interface</strong> - If you've never heard of [IPAWS](https://www.fema.gov/emergency-managers/practitioners/integrated-public-alert-warning-system), it's FEMA's national alerting system which is responsible for those AMBER Alerts you keep ignoring. FEMA provides the system, but the rest is kind of an "assembly required" type of deal. This project involved some fairly complex data modeling and state management, mapping the UI to FEMA's custom [CAP Specification](https://docs.oasis-open.org/emergency/cap/v1.1/ipaws-profile/v1.0/cap-v1.1-ipaws-profile-v1.0.html).

 - <strong>Props, State, Components, Oh My! </strong> - I've written somewhere between dozens and trillions (inclusive) of custom components ranging from base-level "core" components to veritable mini-applications in and of themselves. I am well-versed in [Vue.js](https://vuejs.org/), I know my way around [React](https://react.dev/), and I've even cast a sideways glance or two at [Svelte](https://svelte.dev/).

  - <strong>Migrations and Modernizations, a Myriad</strong> - Over the course of my tenure I have participated in a system-wide frontend redesign, the adoption of modern JavaScript tooling, championed the use of established [design patterns](https://www.patterns.dev/#patterns), and helped to establish and document a consistent design language for our system. 

  - <strong>Oh CRUD</strong> - I've created, read, updated, and deleted so many CRUD applications my head is spinning. I'm the cream of the CRUDS, you might say, and we all know [cream rises to the top](https://www.wwe.com/videos/macho-man-randy-savage-claims-he-s-the-cream-of-the-crop-prime-time-wrestling-may-11-1987).

 This is really just scratching the surface. If you'd like to hear about the more technical aspects for any of these projects, feel free to reach out and we can talk shop.
