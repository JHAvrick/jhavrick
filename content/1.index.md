They say if you utter the word "developers" three times into a mirror it will summon the ghost of Steve Ballmer. I have tried this hundreds of times and it simply does not work. I mean, I asked my buddy about it and he said it works on HIS mirror, but I'm pretty sure he's lying. Wait, what's that? Steve Ballmer is still very much alive? I guess I'll have to try again in thirty or forty years. On the bright side, I've just freed up a few decades! If you or your company would like to get in on that, please do reach out. You can use this blog's [contact form](todo), email me directly at josh@avrick.dev, or send me a message on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joshavrick/).

In the meantime, let me tell you about some of the work I've been doing. You see, when I'm not memorizing passages from my copy of ["The Mythical Man Month"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Mythical_Man-Month), I am working full-time (remotely) at a company called [Alertus](https://www.alertus.com/), based in Baltimore, where we build emergency alerting software, hardware, you name it, *the works*. Here is a (non-exhaustive and unspecific) list of some of the projects for which I have had ownership:

 - <strong>Mass Notification Recipient, a Chrome Extension</strong> - This early project involved the design and development of a new product from scratch, a Chrome extension, which could be deployed by enterprise customers via GSuite (now Google Workspace) or GPO, usually to a fleet of Chromebooks. You'll find some [articles](/articles) on this blog about this.

 - <strong>IPAWS Activation and Reporting, an Interface</strong> - If you've never heard of [IPAWS](https://www.fema.gov/emergency-managers/practitioners/integrated-public-alert-warning-system), it's FEMA's national alerting system which is responsible for all those Amber Alerts you keep ignoring. FEMA provides the system, but the rest is kind of an "assembly required" type of deal. This project involved some fairly complex data modeling and state management, mapping the UI to FEMA's custom [CAP Specification](https://docs.oasis-open.org/emergency/cap/v1.1/ipaws-profile/v1.0/cap-v1.1-ipaws-profile-v1.0.html).

  <!-- - <strong>System States, a Dashboard</strong> - This project  -->

  - <strong>Migrations and Modernizations, a Myriad</strong> - Over the course of my tenure I have participated in a system-wide frontend redesign, the adoption of modern JavaScript tooling, and championed the use of established [design patterns](https://www.patterns.dev/#patterns). 

 - <strong>Props, State, Components, Oh My! </strong> - I've written somewhere between dozens and quadrillions (inclusive) of custom components ranging from base-level "core" components to veritable mini-applications in and of themselves. I am well-versed in [Vue.js](https://vuejs.org/), I know my way around [React](https://react.dev/), and I've even cast a sideways glance or two at [Svelte](https://svelte.dev/).
 
 Hm, what else? I've created, read, updated, and deleted so many CRUD applications my head is spinning. I'm the cream of the CRUDS, you might say, and we all know cream rises to the top. I'm a competent TypeScript developer, though I have not had a chance to use it in a professional context.
